- **5 Calculation Methods**: Full support for major Islamic prayer time calculation methods
  - Umm Al-Qura University (جامعة أم القرى)
  - Muslim World League (رابطة العالم الإسلامي)
  - Islamic Society of North America (جمعية إسلامية)
  - Egyptian General Authority (الهيئة المصرية)
  - Karachi Islamic University (جامعة كراتشي)

- **2 Juristic Schools**: Support for different Islamic schools
  - Standard Method (Shafi'i, Maliki, Hanbali) - Shadow ratio 1:1
  - Hanafi Method - Shadow ratio 1:2

- **7 Prayer Times**: Complete calculation of all Islamic prayer times

  - Asr (العصر) - Afternoon prayer

### Technical Specifications

#### Performance
- **Time Complexity**: O(1) constant time per calculation
- **Space Complexity**: O(1) constant space
- **Execution Time**: ~1 microsecond per calculation
- **No External Dependencies**: Only standard C library (libc, libm)

#### Compatibility
- **Language**: ISO C99
- **Platforms**: Linux, macOS, Unix, Windows (with GCC)
- **Compiler**: GCC 4.8+ or compatible
- **Architecture**: x86, x64, ARM

#### Accuracy
- **Primary Range**: ±1 minute for latitudes -60° to +60° (1900-2100)
- **Extended Range**: ±2-3 minutes for latitudes -66° to +66° (1900-2100)
- **Polar Regions**: ±5+ minutes above 66° latitude
- **Year Range**: Optimized for 1900-2100, extrapolation available

### Known Limitations

- **Polar Regions** (latitude > 66°): Results less reliable, may return midnight for continuous twilight
- **Year Range** (< 1900 or > 2100): Accuracy degrades outside optimal range
- **Timezone Handling**: Supports UTC-12 to UTC+14, no DST adjustment
- **Geographic Model**: Uses spherical Earth approximation, not ellipsoid
